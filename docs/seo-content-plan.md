## Cannibalization guard
- New "noise" piece is **evergreen / legal-mechanism** intent — distinct from the Granbury piece
  which is **location** intent. Different primary keywords, cross-linked, not competing.
- New "how much water" piece is **informational/volume** intent — distinct from rule-of-capture
  (**legal doctrine**) and well-runs-dry (**how-to/action**).
- Abilene piece is the only article targeting that location → no overlap.

## Batch-1 briefs (5 articles)

1. **how-much-water-do-data-centers-use** — harm: water · primary kw: "how much water does a data
   center use texas" · intent: informational. Volume metrics, evaporative cooling, gallons/day,
   what it means for local supply. Links → rule-of-capture, /damage/water.
2. **data-center-noise-texas-nuisance-law** — harm: property · kw: "data center noise nuisance
   texas law" · intent: informational/legal. Low-frequency fan noise, private nuisance, quiet
   enjoyment, what to document. Links → granbury piece, /your-rights, /damage/property.
3. **data-center-subsidence-texas-land** — harm: water · kw: "subsidence data center pumping texas"
   · intent: informational/legal. Aquifer drawdown → land sinking/cracking, why it's actionable
   even under Rule of Capture. Links → rule-of-capture, /your-rights.
4. **abilene-west-texas-data-center-water** — harm: water · location: abilene · kw: "abilene data
   center water" · intent: local. Drought region, scarce supply, well-owner risk. Links →
   /locations/abilene, /damage/water, well-runs-dry.
5. **proving-property-value-loss-data-center** — harm: property · kw: "data center lowered my
   property value texas" · intent: informational/action. Diminution of value, appraisal evidence,
   documenting the loss. Links → /your-rights, /damage/property.

## Compliance (every article)
Soft language only ("may have legal options," "possible avenues"); no guarantees; no invented case
law or statute numbers (Rule of Capture, GCD, TCEQ, HB 49, subsidence are real and may be
referenced generally); mandatory disclaimer block; informational, not legal advice.
